Digital Rights Management (DRM) Policy

*Effective Date: December 5, 2025*

1. Introduction to DRM

Digital Rights Management (DRM) is a technology used by MyLibCom to protect the intellectual property and copyright of the digital content (primarily e-books and licensed material) provided through our platform. This policy outlines the measures we take and your responsibilities as a user concerning this content.

2. DRM Implementation and Measures

We employ various technical measures to prevent unauthorized copying, distribution, or sharing of copyrighted material. These measures include, but are not limited to:

  • **Access Control:** Content access is linked strictly to your registered MyLibCom account.
  • **Digital Watermarking:** Digital purchases may contain a hidden, non-removable watermark identifying the purchasing user's account details.
  • **Device Limits:** Content usage may be limited to a specific number of authorized devices associated with your account.
  • **Download/Print Limitations:** Limitations may be imposed on the number of times a file can be downloaded or printed for certain licensed materials.

3. User Obligations

By accessing and purchasing content on MyLibCom, you agree to the following obligations:

  • You will use the digital content only for your personal, non-commercial use.
  • You will not attempt to bypass, circumvent, or defeat any DRM technology applied to the content.
  • You will not modify, reproduce, distribute, transmit, display, perform, publish, license, create derivative works from, or sell any information or content obtained from the platform.
  • You understand that content ownership remains with the copyright holder and that your purchase grants you a limited, non-exclusive, revocable license to access the content.

4. Consequences of Violation

Violation of this DRM Policy may lead to actions including, but not limited to:

  • Immediate termination of your MyLibCom account and access to all purchased digital content, without refund.
  • Legal action, as violations of copyright law and DRM circumvention may result in significant civil and criminal penalties.

MyLibCom reserves the right to enforce this policy to protect the rights of our authors and publishers.
